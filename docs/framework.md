# The Open Community Leadership Framework

## Principle

Every community creates experiences. The question is whether those experiences are intentional.

Community leadership is not only about running events, managing volunteers, or writing governance documents. It is about designing the conditions that help people feel safe enough to arrive, welcome enough to return, useful enough to contribute, and trusted enough to lead.

---

## The POPCOM Framework


POPCOM is OCL's core framework. It maps the six forces that determine whether a community grows, stagnates, or falls apart from the human side.

| Letter | Dimension | What it means |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **P** | Participation | How people join |
| **O** | Ownership | How people gain responsibility |
| **P** | Purpose | Why people stay |
| **C** | Connection | How relationships form |
| **O** | Opportunity | How contributors grow |
| **M** | Mentorship | How communities reproduce themselves |

POPCOM is not a checklist. It is a lens. Use it to look at your community honestly and not to score yourself, but to see where the gaps are.

Most communities are strong in some dimensions and weak in others. The goal is not perfection. The goal is awareness, and then action.

---

## The experience cycle

This framework is not a ladder. People do not move through communities in one neat direction.

Community experience is cyclical. People arrive, leave, return, volunteer, step back, mentor, disappear, and re-enter years later. POPCOM dimensions are not stages — they are forces that are always present, always active, and always shaping whether someone feels like they belong.

---

## The core question

For every community activity, ask:

> What experience are we creating for the people involved?

Then ask:

- Who feels seen?
- Who feels invisible?
- Who knows how to participate?
- Who is carrying too much?
- Who has a path forward?
- Who might quietly leave?

---

## Stay and leave

People often stay when they feel:

- Seen
- Safe
- Useful
- Connected
- Growing

People often leave when they feel:

- Invisible
- Unsafe
- Unimportant
- Isolated
- Stuck

This does not explain every situation, but it gives organisers a practical starting point.

The experience is what makes people stay. And it is also what makes them leave.
