# Open Community Leadership (OCL)

**Helping communities create future contributors and leaders**

---

Open Community Leadership (OCL) is an open-source project that documents, teaches, and shares practical approaches for building sustainable communities.

OCL focuses on the experiences that help people move from participant to contributor, contributor to organizer, and organizer to future community leader.

The goal is simple: help communities create future contributors and leaders.


These are the skills no school nor company teaches or documents. The skills that live in people, and disappear when those people leave.


OCL gives community leaders a shared language, practical tools, and a place to learn from each other.

The **POPCOM** framework, explores how participation, belonging, mentorship, and community experience help communities create future contributors, organisers, and leaders.

---

## The POPCOM Framework

POPCOM maps the six forces that determine whether a community grows, stagnates, or falls apart.

| | Dimension | What it means |
|--|-----------|---------------|
| **P** | Participation | How people join |
| **O** | Ownership | How people gain responsibility |
| **P** | Purpose | Why people stay |
| **C** | Connection | How relationships form |
| **O** | Opportunity | How contributors grow |
| **M** | Mentorship | How communities reproduce themselves |

→ [Full POPCOM Framework](docs/POPCOM.md)

## How to use this repository

**Self-guided:** Read the modules, complete the exercises, use  and adapt the templates in your own community.

**Workshop:** Run the workshop activities with your organising team or local community.

**Cohort or mentorship program:** Use the modules as a curriculum for a community leadership cohort.
