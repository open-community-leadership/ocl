# Facilitator Guide

## Role of the facilitator

Your role is not to lecture. Your role is to help community leaders notice what is already happening in their communities and turn that into practical action.

## Facilitation principles

- Make the room do the thinking.
- Ask concrete questions.
- Use real examples.
- Avoid abstract leadership jargon.
- Protect psychological safety.
- Do not force people to share sensitive community conflicts.

## Useful prompts

- What did this feel like for the person experiencing it?
- Who benefits from the current setup?
- Who is missing?
- What is the smallest useful improvement?
- What would make someone return?
- What would make someone quietly leave?

## Handling sensitive stories

Participants may share difficult experiences involving exclusion, burnout, conflict, or harm.

Do not ask for names or identifying details. Redirect toward patterns and actions.

Example:

> We do not need to name the person or community. What pattern should organisers learn from this?
