## Why this matters

Connection is what separates a community from an audience. An audience consumes. A community relates.

Belonging is the feeling that comes after connection — the sense that this place is yours, that these people know you exist, that you would be missed if you stopped coming.

Most communities are good at delivering content. Very few are good at designing for connection. This module is about closing that gap.

## What you will learn

- Why content-focused events can accidentally prevent connection
- What belonging actually feels like — and how to design for it
- How to build member-to-member relationships, not just member-to-organiser
- What to do when someone is clearly on the edge of leaving

---

## The connection gap

In many communities, the only person who knows everyone is the organiser. Members know the organiser. They do not know each other.

This is fragile. When the organiser leaves, the community dissolves — because the connections were all routed through one person.

Belonging requires members to know each other. Not perfectly, not deeply — but enough that they would say hello if they passed on the street. Enough that they would notice if someone stopped coming.

---

## Activity: The connection map

Draw a rough map of your community.

Put yourself (or the main organiser) in the centre. Draw lines to every member you can name.

Now draw lines between members who know each other — not through the organiser, but directly.

How dense is the second set of lines compared to the first?

A healthy community has connections running in many directions. A fragile community has all connections running through one point.

---

## Activity: Belonging audit

Think of three people in your community:

1. Someone who has been around for years
2. Someone who joined in the last three months
3. Someone who used to come but stopped

For each person, answer:

- Do they know at least three other members by name?
- Has anyone from the community reached out to them in the last month?
- Do they have a role or responsibility, however small?
- Would they describe the community as theirs?

The answers will tell you where belonging is strong and where it is absent.

---

## Action

Design one moment in your next event that creates member-to-member connection — not member-to-organiser.

This could be: a structured introduction round, a small group conversation, a shared task, an informal space before or after the main event.

The goal is for two people who did not know each other to leave with each other's names.
