## Why this matters

Communities are made of people. People disagree. People hurt each other, sometimes intentionally and often not. How a community handles those moments — or fails to — is one of the most accurate predictors of whether it survives long term.

Safety is not the absence of conflict. It is the presence of trust that conflict can be handled without someone being destroyed by it.

## What you will learn

- Why codes of conduct alone do not create safe communities
- How to handle conflict without losing both people involved
- What psychological safety actually looks like in a volunteer community
- How to have hard conversations before they become crises

## The safety paradox

Communities that avoid all conflict are not safe. They are fragile.

When a community has no mechanism for handling disagreement, the first serious conflict often tears it apart — because no one has practised navigating it. The people involved have no framework. The organisers have no script.

The goal is not a conflict-free community. The goal is a community where conflict is survivable.

---

## What people need to feel safe

- To know the rules are the same for everyone
- To believe that if something goes wrong, someone will respond
- To see that people who cause harm face real consequences
- To feel they can raise a concern without becoming the problem

Most communities have the first two in writing (a code of conduct) and fail at the last two in practice.

---

## Activity: Safety audit

Answer honestly:

- If a new person felt uncomfortable at your last event, would they know what to do?
- If a long-standing member behaved badly, would you handle it the same as if it were a stranger?
- Has anyone ever raised a concern with you — and what happened after?
- Do you know who in your community might be quietly experiencing something difficult right now?

---

## Activity: The hard conversation

Think of a conversation you have been avoiding in your community.

Write down:

- What is the situation?
- What outcome do you want?
- What are you afraid will happen if you have the conversation?
- What will happen if you do not?

Most avoided conversations become larger problems over time. The cost of early discomfort is almost always lower than the cost of late crisis.

---

## Action

Have one conversation you have been avoiding.

It does not have to resolve everything. It has to start.
